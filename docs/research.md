# Research & Technical Foundations

## Overview

PeachBot Core is supported by an ongoing research program focused on the intersection of:

- Edge Artificial Intelligence (Edge AI)  
- Graph Neural Networks (Edge-GNN)  
- Distributed learning systems (FILA)  
- Biologically-inspired computation (SBC)  

The research emphasizes **translation from theory to deployment**, ensuring that models and methods are designed for real-world, resource-constrained environments.

---

## Research Objectives

The core research program is guided by the following objectives:

### 1. Edge-Native Intelligence
Design AI systems that:
- Operate under constrained compute and power conditions  
- Perform real-time inference at the point of data generation  

---

### 2. Resource-Efficient Graph Learning (Edge-GNN)

Develop graph neural network architectures that:
- Can execute on edge devices  
- Maintain performance under limited memory and compute  
- Support biological and relational data modeling  

---

### 3. Distributed Learning Systems (FILA)

Investigate learning architectures that:
- Avoid centralized data collection  
- Enable system-wide intelligence through aggregation  
- Maintain consistency across distributed nodes  

---

### 4. Biologically-Inspired Computation (SBC)

Explore computation models that:
- Incorporate state-aware behavior  
- Adapt continuously to changing environments  
- Reflect principles observed in biological systems

### 1. Edge-AI Systems for Ecological Monitoring

**Title:** Dedicated Edge-AI Single-Board Computer Systems for Ecological Monitoring in Protected Wetlands  
**Domain:** Environmental AI & Edge Computing  
**Date:** January 2026  
**Author:** Swapin Vidya  

**Summary:**  
This work presents the design and deployment of edge-based AI systems for ecological monitoring in a Ramsar-designated wetland. The study focuses on low-power computation, real-time inference, and system reliability in field conditions.
